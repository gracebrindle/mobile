African-American History in Westfield, NJ

I worked with my local historical society and the Martin Luther King Jr. Association to produce a public history of the African-American community in Westfield, New Jersey. The African-American community has historically been underepresented in Westfield's history and this project sought to restore African-American residents and their contributions to the historical narrative.

Unfortunately, very little documentation of the African-American community exists in Westfield. As a result, this project required subaltern methodologies such as reading against the grain to gather evidence and draw conclusions. I recruited and led a team of high school interns dedicated to reading through the town newspaper, The Westfield Leader, from 1910 to the present day to find references to the African-American community. We created a database of relevant articles that we then displayed on an interactive timeline for town residents to access.
